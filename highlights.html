Achievements

Declarations
Championships
For the first time in our team’s history, Team Reaper #22360 advanced to the NYC Championships, one of the most competitive events in the FIRST Tech Challenge circuit, during 2023-2024 season. This milestone marked a huge step forward for our team, showcasing our growth in engineering, strategy, and teamwork. It also brought us just one step away from qualifying for the World Championship.
2nd Place
Inspire Award
We were honored to receive the 2nd Place Inspire Award, one of the most prestigious recognitions in the FTC community, for 2024-2025 first qualifier season. This award celebrates teams that excel in innovation, outreach, community impact, and overall team excellence. It represents the dedication of every member. from builders and programmers to outreach leaders, working together toward one shared goal.

Outreach
Mentorship
Beyond competitions, Team Reaper is dedicated to spreading STEM education. We’ve mentored robotics teams at J.H.S. 157 Halsey, Russell Sage, and P.S. 220 since the team was founded, helping students learn programming, CAD, and engineering design. Through these partnerships, we’ve helped restart and guide multiple robotics programs for younger students in our community.
Outreach
Workshops
We’ve hosted hands-on robotics workshops at the Queens Public Library, giving kids of all ages the chance to explore engineering and coding. Over the summer, we collaborated with Paramount Global to organize a large-scale “Thrive in STEM” event, reaching over 1,000 students alongside companies like Google, Adobe, and Kode with Klossy. These experiences reflect our mission to make STEM learning accessible and inspiring for everyone.

Outreach Activities

Here are activities that we engage younger students to grow their confidence in technologies and inspire them to pursue STEM in their futures

Robot Demonstrations

Show how our FTC robots work, including driving, lifting, and completing challenges to spark kids’ interest in robotics.

Coding Workshops

Teach students the basics of programming using block coding, HTML, and CSS through interactive exercises.

Engineering & CAD Sessions

Guide participants in 3D design and printing activities, helping them understand how robots are planned and built.

Hands-On Robot Challenges

Let kids control mini robots to complete fun tasks like navigating mazes or stacking dominos, giving them a real STEM experience.
